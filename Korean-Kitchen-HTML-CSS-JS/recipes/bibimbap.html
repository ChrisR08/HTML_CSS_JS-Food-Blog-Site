Bibimbap - A classic Korean rice, meat and vegetable dish

Bibimbap, which means "mixed rice" in Korean, is a popular Korean dish that's perfect for a quick and easy weeknight meal or a colorful and flavorful dinner party centerpiece.

This traditional dish features a bed of rice topped with an array of colorful and nutritious ingredients, such as marinated beef, sautéed vegetables, and a fried egg, all served with a spicy gochujang sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 2 courgettes, julienned
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 8 oz. beef sirloin, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 4 tsp. gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 4 tsp. sesame oil
  • 4 tsp. soy sauce
  • Sesame seeds and sliced green onions, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Cook the short-grain rice according to the package instructions.
  2. In a pan, sauté the carrots and zucchinis until tender.
  3. Blanch the bean sprouts in boiling water for 1-2 minutes, then drain.
  4. In a separate pan, stir-fry the beef until browned and cooked through.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together the gochujang, sesame oil, and soy sauce.
  6. Divide the cooked rice among 4 bowls.
  7. Arrange the sautéed vegetables, blanched bean sprouts, and stir-fried beef on top of the rice.
  8. Fry the eggs sunny-side up and place one on top of each bowl.
  9. Drizzle the sauce over each bowl.
  10. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
