Kimbap - Rice and fillings wrapped in seaweed

Kimbap is a popular Korean dish that is known for its vibrant and colorful appearance, as well as its delicious taste. It is a type of Korean sushi that consists of cooked rice and a variety of vegetables, meat, and seafood that are rolled in sheets of seaweed.

Kimbap is a convenient and portable food that is often enjoyed as a snack or a light meal. It can be made with a variety of ingredients to suit different tastes and preferences, and it is a popular dish in Korea and around the world.

Ingredients

  • 5 sheets of roasted seaweed (gim)
  • 3 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • 8 oz. spinach, blanched and squeezed dry
  • 2 medium carrots, julienned
  • 1 small cucumber, julienned
  • 8 oz. imitation crab meat or canned tuna
  • 4 eggs, lightly beaten and scrambled
  • Sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tbsp. sugar
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • Vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix the cooked rice with sugar, salt, rice vinegar, and a drizzle of sesame oil to make the sushi rice.
  2. Lay a sheet of roasted seaweed on a sushi mat or a piece of plastic wrap.
  3. Spread the sushi rice evenly over the seaweed, leaving a 1-inch border at the top edge.
  4. Arrange the spinach, carrots, cucumber, crab meat or tuna, and scrambled eggs on top of the rice.
  5. Using the mat or plastic wrap, tightly roll the seaweed into a cylinder, pressing down gently as you roll to create a firm roll.
  6. Moisten the top border of the seaweed with water to seal the roll.
  7. Repeat with the remaining ingredients to make 5 rolls.
  8. Cut each roll into bite-sized pieces with a sharp knife.
  9. Serve with soy sauce and pickled radish on the side.
